# May 18 Addendum: Stage 4 Confirmation Across the Tier-1-Data Axis — Warsh Confirmation as Operational Phase 5 Marker, Energy-Shock Transmission into CPI and a Corporate Failure

---

## Executive Summary

The April 25–May 18 catch-up batch confirms the Kindleberger Stage 4 state placed on the record by the [April 20 Pressure Clock Firing report](2026-04-20-pressure-clock-firing.md) along a new axis: the axis of Tier 1 hard data. Two developments carry the confirmation. First, the energy shock has transmitted into Tier 1 consumer-price data — the April BLS CPI print of 3.8% annual is a three-year high, with energy +17.9% year-over-year — and into a confirmed corporate failure, the Spirit Airlines wind-down attributed directly to the fuel-cost shock (Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2026; Spirit Airlines, 2026). Second, the Regulatory Phase 5 candidate signal moved from procedural to operational: Kevin Warsh was confirmed Fed Chair 54–45 on May 13, with the Tillis blockade resolved not by Warsh satisfying the disclosure concerns but by the DOJ dropping its probe of Powell (C-SPAN, 2026; Roll Call, 2026).

The load-bearing development is the mechanism of the Warsh confirmation. The executive branch used a DOJ investigation as a procedural lever to seat a Fed chair. That establishes a documented precedent and is precisely the political reversal of the Fed-independence norm the regulatory-cycle framework treats as the principal Phase 5 institutional signal. This moves the Regulatory cycle to the Phase 4/5 boundary — the first Phase 5 marker confirmed operational rather than candidate. It does **not** yet constitute a confirmed Phase 5 enforcement crisis or fraud disclosure; those remain forward-looking.

**No framework phase advances beyond the Regulatory section.** The CPI print deepens Minsky Phase 3 — it does not advance to forced deleveraging. Spirit Airlines is the first confirmed casualty *within* Minsky Phase 3, not a phase advance. The April 24 break condition — HY spread above ~3.5% — remains unmet, at 2.76–2.86%, tighter than the 2.86% reading the April 24 addendum documented. This addendum **confirms and broadens** the April 20 framing; it does not supersede it. It sits parallel to the [April 21 reconfiguration addendum](2026-04-21-pressure-clock-reconfiguration.md) and the [April 24 curve-vs-physical addendum](2026-04-24-curve-vs-physical-divergence.md), anchored to the April 20 parent, not stacked on either.

This is a 14-day accumulation, not a 7-day convergence. Section 4 sets out, explicitly, why the Level 4 convergence trigger does not fire.

## Section 1: The Warsh Confirmation as Operational Phase 5 Marker

The [April 21 reconfiguration addendum](2026-04-21-pressure-clock-reconfiguration.md) Section 2 placed the Phase 5 candidate signal on the Senate Banking Committee procedural record and named three pressure points: the Tillis conditional, the $100M undisclosed-holdings gap, and the DOJ Powell probe. It identified the forward-looking mechanic as a procedural deadlock running against the May 15 statutory clock on Powell's chairmanship. The May 13 confirmation resolves that deadlock, and the *manner* of the resolution is the analytically material fact.

### The mechanism — DOJ probe as procedural lever

The Senate confirmed Warsh 54–45 on May 13 — the most partisan Fed-chair vote on record, with a single Democratic crossover (Fetterman) (C-SPAN, 2026; Roll Call, 2026; Al Jazeera, 2026). The Tillis blockade did not resolve because Warsh satisfied the disclosure questions. The roughly $100M in undisclosed private-fund holdings (The Juggernaut Fund LP, THSDFS LLC) that Democratic senators pressed at the April 21 hearing remained unresolved on the record. The blockade resolved because the DOJ dropped its probe of Powell — the procedural bottleneck the April 21 addendum identified explicitly.

The framework reading is direct. The executive branch used a DOJ investigation as a procedural lever to seat a Fed chair: the probe held the appointment hostage in committee, and the appointment moved when, and only when, the probe was closed. Whatever the merits of the underlying investigation, the *sequence* — DOJ probe live, appointment blocked; DOJ probe closed, appointment cleared — establishes a documented precedent that a DOJ investigation can be opened and closed as an instrument of pressure on Federal Reserve personnel decisions.

### Why this is the principal Phase 5 institutional signal

The regulatory-cycle framework treats the independence of the institutional counterweights that survive late Phase 4 deregulation — the central bank chief among them — as the structure whose political reversal defines the Phase 4→5 transition ([Regulatory Cycle Phases](../theory/regulatory-cycle-phases.md)). The April 15 signal was executive-branch rhetoric against Fed independence. The April 21 record was that rhetoric on a procedural surface. The May 13 confirmation is the first instance in this cycle of the rhetoric producing an *operational* outcome: a Fed chair seated, and seated via a mechanism that subordinated the appointment to an executive-controlled investigation.

This moves the Regulatory cycle from "first Phase 5 candidate signal" to the Phase 4/5 boundary — the first Phase 5 marker confirmed operational rather than candidate. The distinction the framework draws between a candidate signal and a confirmed phase is preserved deliberately here. **This does not yet constitute a confirmed Phase 5 enforcement crisis or a major fraud disclosure** — the two principal Phase 5 firing events named in the escalation tree. Those remain forward-looking. What the May 13 confirmation establishes is the *political-reversal* limb of the Phase 5 definition: the documented reversal of the Fed-independence norm. The enforcement-crisis and fraud-disclosure limbs have not fired.

### Forward-looking mechanic — the June FOMC

The April 21 addendum's forward-looking mechanic — a procedural deadlock against the May 15 clock — is now spent. It is replaced by a discrete, hard binary: Warsh's first FOMC meeting on June 16–17, 2026. Warsh's April 21 hearing vow ("I will not be a sock puppet"; a commitment that "monetary policy independence is essential") is now testable against a 3.8% CPI print (Section 3) that removes the conventional justification for a rate cut. A rate cut, or a clear signal of imminent cuts, against that inflation backdrop would be a discrete Phase 5 confirmation — political capture overriding the price-stability mandate. A hold would leave the Phase 5 question open and the Regulatory cycle parked at the Phase 4/5 boundary this addendum places it at. The June 16–17 meeting is pre-flagged here as the next hard binary on the Regulatory axis and is carried into Section 6.

Powell remains on the Board of Governors through 2028. The confrontation between a Trump-aligned Warsh chair and a Powell-era Board majority is a forward-looking institutional tension; it is noted but not scored.

## Section 2: Energy-Shock Transmission into a Confirmed Corporate Failure — Spirit Airlines

The April 20 parent report documented the alignment-indexed supply shock transmitting into three G7 statutory rationing regimes and, through the April 29 batch, into European airline schedule cuts at scale (Lufthansa 20,000 short-haul routes through October; ~13,000 May flights and ~2 million seats cut sector-wide). The Spirit Airlines wind-down extends that transmission to the first confirmed *corporate failure* — and to the US corporate level.

### The failure and its calibration value

On May 2, Spirit Airlines began an orderly wind-down and cancelled all flights (Spirit Airlines, 2026; NPR, 2026; Al Jazeera, 2026). The stated cause is explicit and quantitative: Spirit's restructuring plan assumed jet fuel at $2.24/gallon in 2026; by late April fuel had reached $4.51/gallon — a 101% deviation from plan. The company stated it required "hundreds of millions of additional dollars of liquidity" it did not have.

The analytical value of the Spirit failure is calibration, not phase advance. It is the first confirmed casualty *within* Minsky Phase 3 — not a signal that the phase has advanced to forced deleveraging at scale. Its specific contribution is that it makes the energy-shock transmission mechanism live and measurable at the US corporate level: a leveraged business plan built on pre-conflict fuel assumptions is insolvent at conflict-level fuel prices, and the arithmetic that sank Spirit ($2.24 plan vs. $4.51 actual) applies, unchanged, to any carrier or logistics operator carrying a pre-conflict cost structure with no balance-sheet buffer. The watch list had tracked fuel-buffer stress as a European concern (Netherlands, Italy, Australia); Spirit shifts the geographic frame to North America and converts the European *schedule-cut* response into a US *cessation* event. Spirit simply had no buffer to absorb what Lufthansa absorbed via schedule reduction.

### Why this is not a Minsky phase advance

The Minsky framework's Phase 3→ forced-deleveraging transition requires collateral calls propagating across institutions — not an isolated corporate failure, however cleanly attributable. Spirit is a single, idiosyncratic failure with a documented external cause. It deepens the Phase 3 read by demonstrating that the zombie-refinancing-failure pattern the watch list tracks now has a confirmed energy-shock vector; it does not advance the phase. Section 6 records Minsky Phase 3 as deepening, unchanged in phase.

## Section 3: Energy-Shock Transmission into Tier 1 Consumer-Price Data — CPI 3.8%

The April BLS Consumer Price Index, released May 12, printed 3.8% annual — a three-year high (highest since May 2023) — with the energy component +17.9% year-over-year and gasoline +28.4% (Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2026; CNBC, 2026; Axios, 2026). This is the first primary-data confirmation that the energy shock has transmitted into Tier 1 consumer-price data.

### What the print does to the Minsky read

The CPI print deepens Minsky Phase 3; it does not advance it. The transmission chain is specific. A 3.8% headline print with energy contributing the bulk of the gain extinguished the 2026 rate-cut path — CME FedWatch now prices zero 2026 cuts and a greater-than-50% probability of a 2027 hike. The rate-cut path was the zombie-refinancing escape valve named in the parent framework: a borrower carrying debt that cannot be serviced at current rates survives only if rates fall. The April CPI print removes that valve and extends the elevated-rate horizon. That is a direct deepening of the Phase 3 refinancing-failure catalyst — it is stagflation pressure short of a Minsky phase transition, not the forced-deleveraging cascade Phase 4 requires.

The core read is worth recording for discipline: core CPI (ex-food and energy) ran +0.4% monthly / +2.8% annual, indicating the energy shock has not yet propagated widely into core goods and services. The pass-through is narrower than the 1973–74 analog. This is consistent with the system-resilience framing in the May 18 Cohen/Forbes narrative artifact (Section 5 below) without negating the structural concern.

### The 30-year breach — a WARN-grade leading indicator, not a Level 4 trigger

The 30-year Treasury yield crossed 5.0% in auction on May 7 — the first time since 2007 (Bloomberg, 2026). This must be stated precisely. The escalation tree's Level 4 immediate trigger names the **10-year** Treasury at 5.0%, not the 30-year. The 30-year breach is a WARN-grade leading indicator: it confirms the directional driver — energy-driven inflation removing all rate-cut optionality — but it is not itself a Level 4 condition. The 10-year reached 4.59–4.63% intra-gap (4.59% on May 15 per Advisor Perspectives) and settled at 4.47%. The 10-year is the instrument the Level 4 trigger is written against, and it has not crossed 4.75% WARN, let alone 5.0%. This addendum does not blur the two: the 30-year print is logged as a leading indicator; the 10-year remains the scored trigger and is carried into Section 6 as the closest-to-trigger Level 4 condition.

### The oil backdrop — no Level 4 oil trigger

For completeness on the energy axis: Brent peaked at $124.24 on April 30 and settled at $106.11 by May 11 (`data/prices.csv`). Brent never sustained above $130/bbl; the Level 4 immediate oil trigger (Brent sustained above $130 for 48+ hours) did not fire at any point in the gap. The IEA's 400-million-barrel coordinated SPR release (announced March 11, 2026; 172 million barrels from the US SPR) is independently confirmed at Tier 1 (International Energy Agency, 2026; U.S. Department of Energy, 2026) — this verifies the figure embedded in the Cohen/Forbes narrative artifact discussed in Section 5. The failed price impact — oil remained elevated through the largest coordinated SPR release in the agency's history — is itself consistent with the April 24 Dallas Fed finding that SPR releases cannot cover a 10.1 mb/d Hormuz disruption.

## Section 4: Why This Is Not Level 4 — The Window Arithmetic

The escalation tree provides a cross-section convergence trigger at Level 4: an armed-conflict signal, a debt signal, and a regulatory signal moving toward the same scenario **within a 7-day window** (or, equivalently, 3+ watch-list indicators from different sections moving toward the same scenario inside that window). The April 25–May 18 batch contains signals in all three sections. It does not meet the trigger. The reason is the window arithmetic, and it is stated here explicitly because the cross-section spread is real and could be mistaken for convergence.

### The span is 14 days, not 7

The armed-conflict escalation in this batch is the Project Freedom escort-under-fire event, dated May 4–5: the US launched Operation Project Freedom on May 4, and only 2 vessels transited successfully under sustained IRGC fire in the first 24 hours. The load-bearing regulatory event is the Warsh confirmation on May 13. The span between them is 8–9 days — outside the 7-day window. The CPI print (May 12) and the Spirit failure (May 2) widen the cluster further: from Spirit on May 2 to the May 14 Trump–Xi summit is a 12-day span. This is a 14-day accumulation, evaluated as a single catch-up batch; it is not a 7-day convergence.

### The signals are not co-directional

The convergence trigger additionally requires the signals to be moving *toward the same scenario*. The April 25–May 18 signals are not co-directional. Project Freedom's escort-under-fire escalation pulls toward escalation (Scenario C). The May 14 Trump–Xi summit — the first direct US–China diplomatic alignment on a Hormuz-reopening objective, with Xi offering Iranian mediation — is de-escalation-adjacent. The two partially offset (Section 5). A cluster whose members point in opposing directions is not a convergence toward a single scenario, which is what the trigger is written to detect.

### No Level 4 immediate trigger fired in the gap

For completeness: no Level 4 *immediate* trigger fired during the April 25–May 18 window. Brent never sustained above $130 (Section 3). The 10-year Treasury did not breach 5.0% (Section 3). There was no derivatives counterparty failure, no G7 sovereign downgrade, no confirmed forced-deleveraging event, no nuclear/radiological event, and no formal Iranian closure declaration or strike on Hormuz physical infrastructure under international law. Project Freedom is a US escort operation under fire — an operational escalation, but not a strike on physical infrastructure and not a formal closure.

The escalation is therefore Level 3 — a set of developments that materially update the geometry and quantitative inputs of the April 20 analysis while the original framing holds. It is delivered as an addendum.

## Section 5: Scenario Table — The Modest May 18 Reweighting

The scenario reweighting in this addendum is modest and rides on the Level 2 watch-list update; it is not an independent escalation trigger. Two co-occurring armed-conflict developments partially offset each other.

| Scenario | April 21 | May 18 | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|
| A: Rapid Resolution | ~8–10% | ~10–12% | Trump–Xi summit + confirmed Chinese transit introduce a Chinese mediation channel; marginally firmer, still capped by stalled enrichment/sovereignty talks |
| B: Managed Naval Standoff | ~30–35% | ~33–37% | Trump–Xi "must remain open" alignment pulls toward a managed equilibrium; partially offset by Project Freedom converting the standoff into an active escort engagement |
| C: Mine or Naval Incident | 35–40% | ~33–38% | Project Freedom escort-under-fire keeps kinetic context live; marginally eased by the diplomatic channel |
| D: Great-Power Incident | ~20–25% | ~18–22% | Xi's assurance against supplying Iran military equipment, if operationally confirmed, narrows one Scenario D pathway |

The largest single move is approximately 3–5 percentage points. This is well under the 15-percentage-point Level 2 scenario-shift threshold and far under any Level 4 condition. It is not a regime change. The Trump–Xi summit and the confirmed Chinese transit pull toward de-escalation; Project Freedom under fire pulls toward escalation; they partially offset, and the table moves modestly. The reweighting is recorded in the watch-list Armed Conflict scenario table as a new May 18 column.

A note on the Chinese-axis indicator. The April 21 addendum and the watch list both carried the Chinese axis as "untested." The May 13–14 Chinese-linked tanker transits partially resolve it: the transits occurred under Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority (PGSA) toll framework — an Iranian administrative arrangement, not a US-sanctioned safe corridor and not a US-escorted transit. The question of whether China-flagged tonnage would *attempt* a transit is now answered (yes); the question of whether the US would *enforce its blockade against* China-flagged tonnage remains untested, because these transits were Iranian-permitted rather than US-escorted. The indicator moves from "untested" to "partially resolved" on those terms.

## Section 6: What Breaks This Read

Four conditions, any of which would invalidate or supersede the framing in this addendum. The first is carried unchanged from the April 24 addendum and is the single most important.

### 1. HY spread crossing ~3.5%

Section 5 of the April 24 addendum named 3.5–4.0% high-yield spread as the Kindleberger Stage 5 engage threshold and documented HY at 2.86%. As of the current `data/prices.csv` pull, HY is at 2.76–2.86% — tighter than the April 24 reading, not wider. If HY widens above ~3.5%, the diagnostic flips from Stage 4 confirmation to Stage 5 onset, and a Level 4 full-report escalation is warranted immediately — the credit-structure break combined with the Tier-1-data confirmation this addendum documents is a materially different analytical condition than either signal in isolation. This remains the single most important break condition for the entire April 20 lineage.

### 2. 10-year Treasury crossing 5.0%

The 10-year Treasury at 5.0% is a standalone Level 4 immediate trigger in the escalation tree. With the 10-year settled at 4.47% (intra-gap 4.59–4.63%) and the directional driver — energy-driven inflation, zero rate-cut optionality — now confirmed in Tier 1 data, and with the 30-year already through 5.0%, the 10-year is closer to its Level 4 trigger than any other Level 4 condition. A breach is a standalone full-report escalation.

### 3. A second airline or logistics failure within ~14 days of Spirit

Spirit Airlines is currently read as the first confirmed casualty *within* Minsky Phase 3 (Section 2). A second airline or logistics-sector failure within roughly 14 days of the Spirit wind-down would convert that read from "first casualty within Phase 3" to "leading edge of a cascade" — collateral and cost-structure stress propagating across operators — which is the Minsky Phase 3→4 pattern and would reopen the Level 4 question.

### 4. The June 16–17 FOMC

Warsh's first FOMC meeting on June 16–17 is the next hard binary on the Regulatory axis (Section 1). A rate cut, or a clear signal of imminent cuts, against the 3.8% CPI print would be a discrete, hard Phase 5 confirmation — political capture overriding the price-stability mandate — and would advance the Regulatory cycle from the Phase 4/5 boundary into Phase 5. A hold leaves the Phase 5 question open.

### What does not count as a break

A further Brent decline from the $106 level without mine-clearance progress. A resumption of US–Iran or Xi-mediated talks without a verified safe corridor. A single additional European schedule-cut announcement. These are surface-level repricing events inside the existing framing, not break conditions for it.
