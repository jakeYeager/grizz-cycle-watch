## Section 1: Goldman Prime Brokerage Completes the Kindleberger Stage 4 Diagnostic

The April 13 convergence report called Stage 4 confirmed across the private credit sector based on the gate cascade: all four major alternative credit platforms in simultaneous gate mode, with Fitch's 5.8% Q1 default rate confirming zombie refinancing failures as the underlying cause. The report documented the credit tightening side of the escalation-tree's Stage 4 phase-shift trigger.

The escalation-tree's Stage 4 trigger requires two conditions met simultaneously: "documented smart money exit + simultaneous credit tightening." The April 13 report had the credit tightening. The Goldman prime brokerage data logged April 14 provides the equity-side confirmation of the first condition.

The Goldman data is quantified and sustained, not a 48-hour whiplash trade:

- Hedge funds sold global equities at the fastest pace in 13 years in March 2026
- Short-to-long ratio: 7.6:1 — shorts running at their highest level since September 2022
- Six consecutive weeks of net equity exposure reduction (Goldman prime brokerage desk reporting "capitulation signs")
- US hedge fund average drawdown in March: 4.3%; Asia-focused funds: 7.3% — worst monthly drawdown since January 2022
- European macro-linked shorts at decade highs
- Simultaneous institutional accumulation of fat-tail put options at $4,950–$5,050 SPX strikes, explicitly hedging against Hormuz escalation scenarios; SPX put/call ratio reached 1.18 on April 10

The signal quality distinguishes this from noise. Six consecutive weeks of net selling across funds with different investment strategies, geographies, and investor bases is a sustained directional commitment, not position management. The simultaneous put buying at specific Hormuz-correlated strike prices confirms that institutional actors are explicitly pricing a tail scenario the retail narrative is dismissing.

The divergence from the retail narrative is the Stage 4 pattern itself. On April 13, CNBC and Jim Cramer told a mass retail audience that Hormuz is irrelevant to stock valuations and that rates are "the only thing that matters." That broadcast aired the same day Goldman's prime brokerage clients were running 7.6:1 short exposure and buying puts against a Hormuz black swan. The [Kindleberger Mania Model](../market-behavior/kindleberger-mania-model.md) places the Stage 4 "distress" signal precisely here: smart money exits while retail "buys the dip," credit tightens, and the media narrative maximizes retail exposure at the inflection point.

The watch list's Stage 4 indicator — "quiet institutional selling while retail 'buys the dip' — divergence between smart money flows and retail sentiment" — is now quantified on both sides of the divergence simultaneously. The April 13 report had the credit side; the Goldman data provides the equity side. Both conditions of the escalation-tree phase-shift trigger are now explicitly evidenced. The Kindleberger Stage 4 diagnostic is complete across sectors.

## Section 2: China's Ambiguity Resolved to the Escalation Column

The April 13 convergence report's Section 5 held China's dual posture explicitly unresolved: verbal restraint (Foreign Ministry "calm and restraint" statement, April 13) running alongside military presence (PLA 48th fleet in theater since March, Maritime Security Belt 2026 exercises with Russian and Iranian forces). The report characterized this as "China preserving maximum optionality" and noted that the watch list's de-escalation signal requires Beijing to back the mine-clearance framework specifically — which China had not done. Both the de-escalation rhetoric and the escalation military signal were active simultaneously.

As of April 14, that ambiguity has resolved to the escalation column. Three developments, logged together:

**Chinese state media escalation.** China's Foreign Ministry escalated from April 13's "calm and restraint" to April 14's formal characterization of the US blockade as "dangerous and irresponsible" — a materially sharper official register. This directly meets the watch list's escalation indicator: "Chinese state media escalates rhetoric."

**Explicit transit intent declared.** China simultaneously stated it will "continue to use the Strait of Hormuz, fulfill energy deals with Iran, and maintain the presence of its navy in the region," with a formal warning against US interference with that stated intent. This is not a diplomatic formulation — it is a public operational declaration that China intends to test the US interdiction line.

**UN Security Council veto.** China vetoed the April 7 UN Security Council resolution placing blame on Iran, foreclosing the multilateral diplomatic channel and confirming that China is actively protecting Iran's position in the international framework rather than seeking a neutral mediating posture.

The watch list indicator table has two Chinese signal types:

| Signal type | Indicator | Status before April 14 | Status April 14 |
|---|---|---|---|
| De-escalation | Beijing publicly calls for restraint; backs mine-clearance framework | Partially met (restraint rhetoric, no mine-clearance backing) | Not met — rhetoric reversed |
| Escalation | PLA Navy deployment orders; Chinese state media escalates rhetoric | Partially met (PLA fleet present; rhetoric was verbal restraint) | Fully met — both conditions active |

The de-escalation optionality that Section 5 described as "preserved" is now closed. China's public position is a direct collision course with the US interdiction order on a declared intent basis. The April 13 convergence report assessed Scenario D at 12% with Chinese ambiguity unresolved. With both conditions of the escalation column met and the de-escalation path reversed, Scenario D probability moves directionally higher.

## Section 3: India Identified as the Near-Term Scenario D Catalyst

The [Three Blockade Problem](../armed-conflict/three-blockade-problem.md) named "Chinese/Indian vessel attempting Hormuz transit under bilateral deal; US interdiction orders tested" as the primary watch list indicator for Scenario D escalation. The report identified India's Operation Urja Suraksha (five frontline warships deployed to escort Indian vessels) and documented the direct collision geometry: a US naval commander enforcing the blockade order has no discretionary authority to wave through an Indian state-flagged tanker. The Three Blockade Problem noted this without specifying which actor moves first or what domestic pressure mechanism drives the decision to attempt transit.

The April 14 India signal log entry provides that specification. India is now the higher-probability near-term actor, and the domestic pressure mechanism is visible and operating at scale.

### The energy double-bind

India faces simultaneous closure of both major emergency supply channels:

- The Hormuz closure has removed approximately 65% of India's crude import volume from the table
- The 30-day US OFAC waiver allowing India to purchase stranded Russian crude — issued March 5–6 as a bridge mechanism — expired approximately April 4–5 without confirmed extension, days before the US naval blockade hardened on April 12

The two closures compound. India cannot route back through Hormuz; cannot draw on Russian crude under the OFAC framework; and the Saudi East-West pipeline offset that reduced worst-case Brent range does not address India's import geography. Standard Chartered cut India's FY2027 GDP growth forecast to 6.4% from 7.0% on energy disruption alone. Each $10/bbl oil price increase raises India's import bill $13–14 billion, pushes the current account deficit 30–40 basis points, and reduces GDP by approximately 0.5 percentage points.

### LPG as the specific pressure vector

The energy shortage is not abstract. The specific commodity in visible shortage is LPG — cooking gas, the fuel used for daily household use across India's lower-income households. India imports approximately 60% of its LPG through Hormuz. Black market LPG cylinder prices have surged 3–5x: from ₹900–1,200 to ₹3,200–4,000 per standard 14.2-kg cylinder.

The Indian government's official position — "no shortage" — is contradicted by the black market pricing and by the labor market impact already visible at scale:

- More than 150,000 textile workers have left Surat, Gujarat
- More than 500,000 workers impacted in Morbi ceramics/tiles cluster
- 100+ factories shuttered in Gujarat manufacturing centers
- The displacement mirrors the COVID-19 reverse migration of 2020 in speed and scale

The Phase 3/4 institutional credibility pattern is present: official denial of a visible, large-scale disruption. The government cannot sustain the "no shortage" position if the labor market displacement continues to compound.

### Why India moves first

China has the stronger declared intent — a formal statement that it will continue Hormuz transit — but China's calculus is governed by strategic patience and the full weight of US-China economic interdependence. China has the option to wait and let India absorb the initial interdiction risk.

India's calculus is different. A democratic government facing visible, large-scale displacement of hundreds of thousands of workers has strong, near-term electoral and social pressure incentives to act. India's Operation Urja Suraksha naval deployment is already in place — the escort vessels are already in theater. The decision India faces is not whether to deploy forces but whether to use the forces already deployed to attempt a bilateral transit arrangement with Iran.

The pressure clock is running. The watch list indicator "Indian vessel attempting Hormuz transit under bilateral deal" now has a visible domestic mechanism — LPG shortages, mass worker displacement, government credibility pressure — that was not documented in the Three Blockade Problem's original scenario table.

### Near-term trigger to watch

India formally requests or executes a bilateral Hormuz transit arrangement with Iran before the April 21 ceasefire expiry. If that attempt occurs before a second US-Iran talks session has been convened, it tests the US interdiction line simultaneously with a potential diplomatic window — the most dangerous combination for Scenario D escalation. A second US-Iran meeting would introduce a brief de-escalatory signal that could defer the transit attempt; the absence of a second meeting by April 18–19 substantially increases the probability that India moves on its own timeline.
