### Blue Owl Capital caps private credit fund redemptions at 5%

**Indicators:** Private equity NAV markdowns or redemption gates at major funds; shadow banking stress events
**Sections:** Market Behavior, Global Debt
**Scenario impact:** Confirms concealed distress in private credit; adds Minsky Phase 2→3 transition evidence — small shock propagating into forced deleveraging
**Source:** Bloomberg, CNBC, Semafor, April 2, 2026
**Note:** Blue Owl's flagship OCIC fund received redemption requests of 21.9% of shares outstanding in Q1; its OTIC fund received 40.7%. Both capped at 5%. Blue Owl simultaneously sold $1.4bn of direct lending assets to boost liquidity; Goldman Sachs private credit fund narrowly avoided a comparable gate. The redemption gate mechanism is now active at scale across private credit — a market that holds a substantial portion of corporate zombie debt that cannot survive elevated rates. This is not an isolated event; it is a structural signal that concealed distress is becoming visible.
