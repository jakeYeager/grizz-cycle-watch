# Source Quality Standards

Applies to all agents when evaluating sources for signal log inclusion.

---

## Tier 1 — Primary sources (highest weight)

- Government statements, official agency releases, legislative text
- Central bank publications (Fed, ECB, BIS, IMO, IAEA)
- Shipping data: AIS vessel tracking, MARAD advisories, port authority reports
- Official exchange data: FRED, commodity exchange settlement prices
- Company filings, earnings releases, regulatory disclosures (SEC, FCA)

## Tier 2 — Institutional analysis (use with attribution)

- Major bank research (J.P. Morgan, Goldman Sachs, BIS Quarterly Review)
- Established financial wire services: Bloomberg, Reuters, FT
- Specialist trade publications: gCaptain, Lloyd's List, Platts, ICIS

## Tier 3 — General news (use for event confirmation only)

- Major national outlets (WSJ, NYT, BBC, Al Jazeera, CNBC, NPR)
- Acceptable for confirming that an event occurred; not sufficient alone for quantitative claims

## Not acceptable as sole source

- Opinion columns, op-eds, substack posts
- Social media posts (including official accounts — confirm via Tier 1/2)
- Aggregators without original reporting (Yahoo Finance summaries, etc.)
- Wikipedia (acceptable for background context, not for primary event claims)

---

## Application

When logging a signal, prefer the highest-tier source available. If only Tier 3 is available, note it in the Source field and flag that confirmation from a primary source is pending.

Cross-cutting signals (multiple sections moving simultaneously) warrant extra sourcing diligence — a single Tier 3 source is insufficient for a Level 4 escalation recommendation.
